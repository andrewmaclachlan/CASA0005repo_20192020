11.11 Basics

  • Don’t cut and paste maps / figures, export them propperly
  • Make maps and figures the right way — don’t just leave layer names (e.g. osm_points_road) in a legend, rename them.
  • If you use a formula write it properly — don’t cut and paste
  • Make sure you write a sufficient caption for figures / maps / flow diagrams / tables. The reader should be able to understand them without any other information. In other words, if you gave someone a figure with just the caption they should be able to tell you what it shows
  • Don’t bullet point methods, summarise with sentances and use a flow diagram if appropraite

Here is a quick guide to writing a report…

Make sure you say why your mini investiation is important near the start— why should we care! The introduction should set the scene and introduce the reader to issue, its importance and lead onto outlining the research question of the study.

The literature review should evaluate exisiting research, deomstrate contrasting and/or similar views whilst highlight research gaps (that hopefully your research will contribute to / address). This could also include policy documents or documents from reputable soruces (e.g. UN / EU / authoritative bodies).

Methodology is what you did (analysis, data processing etc), a reader should be able to replicate your analysis but you don’t need excessive detail — you do not need to list every tool you used. For example, data was loaded uisng read_csv() and then reprojected using st_transform() is too much information for the assignment (you could include this type of information in the comments of your code / RMarkdown document).

Results is what you found — be selective about figures you include a narrative guding the reader through your findings, don’t just present result after result after result.

Discussion should have lots of critical reflection…by that i mean… how do your results fit in with and then advance the ideas presented by others either in academic or policy literature. Really say why this is important with appropraite referencing.

Your conclusion should restate what you set out to investigate, summarise how you acheived it, what your results showed, why it is important and in this case specifying recommendations (e.g. what should be changed or altered to fix the issue you explored).
